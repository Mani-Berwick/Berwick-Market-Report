What This Means

Less choice, faster sales, stronger prices.

Put plainly: there are fewer homes on the market than there were this time last year, but the same number of buyers are still out looking. That imbalance is exactly what's pushing days on market down and clearance rates up — homes are selling faster, and more of them are selling for a strong price rather than being passed in.

"When stock is tight, the homes that present well and are priced on evidence are the ones buyers compete for — not just the ones that are merely available."

For sellers, that's a genuinely good window. For buyers, it means moving with more certainty once you've found the right place, because hesitation is costing people the home they wanted.

If you're thinking of selling

Fewer competitors, more attention

With stock this tight, your home faces less direct competition for buyer attention — which is exactly the kind of market where a well-presented home can outperform the median.
